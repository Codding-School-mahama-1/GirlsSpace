Sarah Johnson

EcoPack Solutions

While studying environmental science, Sarah noticed the excessive plastic waste on campus. She launched EcoPack, creating sustainable packaging from agricultural waste. Within two years, her company secured contracts with major campus vendors and expanded to three other universities.

Advice: "Start with a problem you're passionate about solving. The business aspect follows naturally when you genuinely care about your mission."

Marcus Chen

StudyStream App

As a computer science major struggling with focus, Marcus developed StudyStream - an app that connects students for virtual study sessions. The app gained 50,000 users in its first year and now features AI-powered productivity tracking.

Advice: "Build something you need yourself. Your personal pain points can reveal business opportunities that resonate with others."

Jasmine Rodriguez

Campus Connect

Jasmine noticed international students struggling to adapt to campus life. She created Campus Connect, a platform that pairs international students with local mentors. The service now operates on 12 campuses and has helped over 5,000 students.

Advice: "Don't wait for the perfect idea. Start with what you have, learn from feedback, and iterate quickly. Progress beats perfection every time."

Essential Advice for Student Entrepreneurs

Validate Your Idea

Test your concept with a small group before investing significant time and resources.

Manage Time Wisely

Create a strict schedule that balances academic responsibilities with business development.

Build Your Network

Leverage campus resources, professors, and fellow students for support and guidance.

Bootstrap When Possible

Start with minimal investment and reinvest profits to grow your business organically.
